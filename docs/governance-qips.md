# QIPs & Governance

**QRL Improvement Proposals (QIPs)** are the community process for changing the QRL **core protocol** and other system-wide components ([QIP overview](https://docs.theqrl.org/build/qip/overview)).

## Scope

QIPs cover layers such as:

- **core** — consensus changes requiring forks
- **networking** — P2P and related infrastructure
- **security** — cryptographic and hardening changes

Client-only or non-global proposals may route through complementary programs (e.g. QEP-style processes mentioned in the overview) ([QIP overview](https://docs.theqrl.org/build/qip/overview)).

## Lifecycle (summary)

1. Draft discussion with authors and community.
2. Custodians (**Jack Matier**, **James Gordon**, **JP Lomas** as listed in docs) review readiness.
3. Promotion to **proposal** with on-chain governance parameters ([qip-workflow](https://docs.theqrl.org/build/qip/qip-workflow)).

## How to participate

The official docs encourage:

- joining design discussions,
- staying informed beyond summaries,
- voting when governance windows open ([QIP overview](https://docs.theqrl.org/build/qip/overview)).
